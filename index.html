Bb Tenor Trombone.

The basic trombone is the standard Bb Tenor Trombone. This straight trombone has been around since the early 17th century and its design hasn't really changed; apart from it growing in size. These trombones are great for beginners lightweight and great value for money. Although it's not just for beginners as some people like the fact these are the most open sounding.

  1. no trigger

  2. Low Bb to 3RD Bb
  3. One of the smaller Trombones'

F-Attachment.

The F Attachment allows for greater flexibility in how to approach the lower ranges of the trombone. While a standard tenor trombone can only go to E under the staff and the lower pedal tone series, F attachment provides a bridge to the pedal range.

  • Has a trigger in F

  • 2ND Low Bb to 3RD high Bb
  • Is one of the biggest Trombone's

Bass Trombone

  • 3rd Low Bb to 2nd high Bb
  • Has two triggers
  • The biggest Trombone in the Trombone family
